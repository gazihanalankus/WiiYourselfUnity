WiiYourself In Unity

WiiYourself in Unity!

WiiYourself Unity is an unmanaged windows only library (.dll) and C-Sharp interop Bridge class written by Lonewolfwilliams that wraps the WiiYourself library written by gl.tter

Good news

This is not a plugin, which means that this library can be used with the free edition of Unity.

This project is distributed for free under a BSD licence

Bad news

This is for windows only

I don't provide support for this library, after all, it didn't cost you anything ;)

Using the library

Although the sample project included here should work 'out of the box' you need to pair a WiiMote with Windows first

Unfortunately pairing your WiiMote successfully with windows depends on the compatibility of your WiiMote with your bluetooth stack

Some people seem to have more success with the Blue Soleil stack, than their proprietary one

Pairing your Bluetooth controller is done as follows:

  1. Click 'start' and then click 'control panel'
  2. Under hardware and sound click 'add a device' (you may have the 'small icons' view if so click 'devices and printers' then 'add a device')
  3. Hold down the 1 and 2 buttons (keep them held down through the whole pairing process)
  4. Follow the instructions on the 'add a device' wizard (It may take a couple of seconds for the device to be visible)
  5. The Wiimote should show up as: 'Nintendo RVL-CNT-01' or simply 'Input Device'
  6. If you are asked, pair without using a code
  7. When the system tray shows that the hardware has been installed you may release buttons 1 and 2

Troubleshooting

  • Q: My WiiMote doesn't show up in 'add a device'
    • A: Your proprietary bluetooth stack may not be very compatible with your WiiMote, you could try using Blue Soleil
    • A: Make sure that you are holding down the 1 and 2 buttons on your WiiMote throughout the pairing process
    • A: Make sure your bluetooth is switched on
    • A: Make sure that your WiiMote is charged
  • Q: My WiiMote is paired but doesn't seem to be recognised when I run the unity project
    • A: Try removing your WiiMote from the device list (click 'devices and printers' then right click on 'Nintendo RVL-CNT-01' and select 'Remove device' (you will have to go through the pairing process again) One paired, try re-opening and running the Unity Project
  • Q: My Wii is wobbly
    • A: The input from the accelerometers is very precise (or sensitive depending on how you look at it) you may want to use some kind of smoothing algorithm

Further Development

At the moment only a small amount of gl.tters library is exposed via my managed wrapper, which is currently only really a proof of concept - you can set LED's on the WiiMote (to test outgoing communication) and you can get the pitch roll and 'directional' value from the Wiimote (to test incoming communication)

I hope to continue development on this project wrapping more functionality over time (I'm particularly interested in the sound capabilities) or you could help me speed up development by submitting a patch ;)
